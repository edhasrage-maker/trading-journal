# Validation / Discovery Kit — Acuity

*Goal: prove (or kill) the idea before sinking ~3 months into the build. Copy-paste
templates below. Methodology: The Mom Test — talk about their life, not your idea;
dig into past behavior, not hypotheticals; commitments are signal, compliments are noise.*

---

## 0. The one rule

**Never pitch during discovery.** The moment you describe Acuity, people get polite and
lie to spare your feelings. You learn nothing. Discovery first (their world), demo second
(watch them use it), ask second (commitment). Compliments ("cool idea!", "I'd totally use
that!") are **worthless** — only time, money, or reputation put on the line counts.

---

## 1. The go/no-go gate (decide BEFORE you start, so you can't move the goalposts)

Proceed to the multi-tenant build **only if, out of ~8–10 target traders:**

- **≥ 5** describe an unprompted, real pain with reviewing/improving their trading, AND
- **≥ 3** actively use the current app *on their own* for at least a few sessions, AND
- **≥ 3** make a **hard commitment** — not "I'd pay," but one of:
  - prepays an annual/early-access plan (even discounted), or
  - puts down a refundable deposit for a beta seat, or
  - signs a written "I'll pay $X/mo at launch" + intro's you to 2 other traders.

**Weak signals that do NOT count:** "looks great," "send me the link," "definitely when
it's ready," social-media likes. If you only get those → iterate positioning or pause.
**This gate protects your scarcest asset: time.**

---

## 2. Who to target (build a list of 8–10)

**Ideal profile:**
- Serious / semi-pro futures trader (or prop-firm-eval trader)
- Trades ES/NQ/CL/etc. on NinjaTrader, Tradovate, Sierra, TradingView, Quantower
- Has tried to journal and either grinds at it manually or quit
- Bonus: has paid for Tradezella / TraderSync / Edgewonk (proves they buy tools)

**Where to find them:**
- Your own contacts / trading friends (start here — warmest)
- Prop-firm Discords (Topstep, Apex, MyFundedFutures, etc.)
- Futures-trading subreddits / X (Twitter) futures community
- Local/online trading groups you're already in

**Tracking table — keep this updated:**

| Name/handle | Source | Platform/broker | Journals today? | Pays for tools? | Stage | Commitment? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| | | | | | reached out / interviewed / on app / committed | |

## 3. Outreach templates

**A) Warm (friends / known traders)**
> Hey [name] — I'm building a trading-analytics tool for futures traders and I want
> brutally honest feedback before I waste months building the wrong thing. Could I grab
> 15 min to hear how you review your trading? Not selling you anything — I genuinely just
> want to learn how you do it today. This week work?

**B) Semi-cold (community member)**
> Hi [name] — saw your posts in [community]. I'm a futures trader building a journaling/
> analytics tool and doing research with other serious traders. Not pitching anything —
> I just want to understand how you analyze your sessions. Open to a quick 15-min call,
> or even a few replies here?

**C) The follow-up to put them on the app (only after a good interview)**
> This was super helpful. I've actually got a working version I use daily — would you be
> up for trying it with your own trades for a few days and telling me where it sucks?

*Tone: curious, humble, zero hype. You're a researcher, not a salesperson.*

---

## 4. The interview script (~15 min, discovery BEFORE any demo)

**Frame it (10 sec):** "I'm not selling anything — I just want to learn how you actually
review your trading. There are no right answers."

**Their current world (the gold is here):**
1. "Walk me through what you do *after* a trading session today — step by step."
2. "When did you last sit down and journal or review? What did that actually look like?"
   *(If 'I don't really' → why not? That's the real story.)*
3. "What are you trying to figure out when you review — what question are you answering?"

**The pain (dig, don't lead):**
4. "Last time you hit a losing stretch, how did you figure out what went wrong?"
5. "What's the most frustrating part of understanding your own trading?"
6. "Have you tried journaling tools? Which ones? …Why did you stop / keep using it?"
   *(Abandonment stories are pure gold — they reveal what 'useful' really means.)*

**Money (factual, about the past — not hypothetical):**
7. "What do you pay right now for trading tools — data, platforms, journals, eval fees?"
8. "What did [Tradezella/their tool] cost? Was it worth it? What made it worth/not worth it?"

**Avoid these (they produce lies):**
- ❌ "Would you use a tool that…?" → hypothetical, everyone says yes
- ❌ "Do you think this is a good idea?" → fishing for compliments
- ❌ Anything that describes Acuity before they've talked

---

## 5. The concierge demo (watch, don't narrate)

After discovery, get them on the *current app* with **their own trades**.
- **Stay quiet.** Don't explain. Watch where they get stuck, what they ignore, what makes
  them lean in. Their face at the MFE/MAE / capture stat is your real product feedback.
- Listen for the magic moment: do they react to seeing day-one insight (capture %, condition
  edge) the way the pitch promises? If not, the differentiator isn't landing — that's vital.
- Afterward: "What, if anything, was useful? What was confusing? What's missing before
  you'd use this for real?"

---

## 6. The commitment test (the only result that matters)

Don't ask "would you pay?" Instead, offer something real and watch what they *do*:
- "I'm opening a few early-access seats at $X/mo (or $Y/yr), locked in for life. Want one?"
- Or a refundable deposit to hold a beta seat.
- Or: "Who are two other traders you'd tell about this?" (referrals = belief)

**Price-anchoring probe:** "Based on what you pay for [their current tools], what would make
this a no-brainer at $20/mo? What would make you say it's too expensive?" — listen for the
real ceiling, not a polite number.

---

## 7. What to record after each conversation

For each trader, one line: **biggest pain (their words) · what they pay now · reaction to
the day-one demo · commitment (yes/no/what) · would-refer (names).**

Patterns across 8–10 of these tell you: is the pain real, is the day-one wedge the actual
hook, and what's the true price ceiling.

---

## 8. Timeline (at ~5–10h/wk)

- **Week 1:** build the list (8–10), send outreach A/B.
- **Weeks 2–3:** run interviews (aim 6–8). Start putting the best fits on the app.
- **Week 4:** concierge demos + commitment asks → **score against the gate (Section 1).**
- **Decision:** gate passed → start the build; gate failed → iterate positioning or pause.

Total: ~1 month of light effort to de-risk a ~3-month build. Best trade you can make.
